One Punch Man
Sunday, January 10th, 2016

Into a Dragon Ball Z or Marvel Universe world, run by superbeings with various thematic powers, who battle each other by shouting out the names of their moves, is inserted Saitama, the bald, everyman protagonist.

He's an amateur superhero. His power is to simply kill anyone in a single punch, which he uses on the monsters that are endlessly tearing down buildings and devouring apartment dwellers in his hometown, City Z. (There is also City A, City B, etc. which appear to comprise the rest of the world). A running gag is to introduce a huge, stunningly-drawn monster, linger over the destruction in their wake, continue as they explain their origins and plans for our subjugation, and then have Saitama cut them off mid-sentence with a punch.

The joke is always the same, but I always laughed. The monsters are so beautifully grotesque and elaborate and their plans so earnest.

Sometimes monsters will survive long enough to start arcs, which keeps you on your toes.

The world of One Punch Man runs on individuals' "power", which is explicitly quantifiable even though it takes various forms: telekinesis, having a magic sword, lightning feet, shooting fireballs, having ghoulishly large muscles, ability to survive being punched into a mountain. Superheroes are regulated by the Heroes Association, which ranks them from A1 to C390, plus the super-elite S-class above them all.
