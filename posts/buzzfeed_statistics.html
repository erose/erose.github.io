Buzzfeed Messed Up Some Statistics And What That Means About The World
Tuesday, January 8th, 2019

This article, about millenials and burnout, contains the following assertion (at the time of writing this post):

The planet is dying. Democracy is under serious threat. American adults report being 39% more anxious than a year ago...

The link goes to poll in which the American Psychiatric Association talked to 1,000 American adults. Below are reproduced the results.

In general, are you more or less anxious than you were at this time last year?

  • More anxious 39%
  • Less anxious 19%
  • About the same 39%
  • Not sure 3%

The error is that Buzzfeed has confused "39% of adults say they are more anxious" with "adults are 39% more anxious." For example, 100% of Americans are older than they were last year, but Americans are not 100% older than they were last year. Come on, guys.

A 39% increase in anything on a survey like this should be large enough, I think, to merit a double take. That's how my friend and I found this error -- we looked at it, and said "Really?". I guess I see how this wording or even the whole distinction between "39% had some increase" and "39% increase" could slip if you're the author with your mind on a billion things. But how many people at Buzzfeed read this article before it went to print? How many of them looked at this number and did not do a double take? How could they have missed it?
