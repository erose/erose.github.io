Fake Palindromes by Andrew Bird
Thursday, April 11th, 2019

Today I'll be analyzing the song Fake Palindromes by Andrew Bird, from his 2005 album Andrew Bird & The Mysterious Production of Eggs.

I think everyone who has ever listened to Fake Palindromes has had two thoughts:

  • "Wow, this song is so beautiful!"
  • (some time later) "Wow, these lyrics are really fucked up."*

Fake Palindromes is a waterfall of a song, powerful and smooth. Like most Andrew Bird tracks, it is the opposite of lo-fi -- there are no seams in the world he creates out of soft-spoken voice and violin. Just like water molecules stick tightly to each other, so do the singer's words, which rhyme so many times per line it can be hard to parse them individually. This song could be used in a car ad.

When you first understand the words being said, they mostly just sound random and word-salad-y. On later listens, they cohere into. I don't think you're missing too much if you never get to this step

  My dewey-eyed Disney bride, what has tried
  Swapping your blood with formaldehyde?
  Monsters? Whiskey-plied voices cried fratricide
  Jesus, don't you know that you could have died?

* See, for example, its entry in a blog series called Secretly Horrifying Song Lyrics.
