On Motivated Moral Realism
Friday, August 30th, 2019

When I was young, I believed that good and bad were part of the fabric of the world. I believed that questions like "Is it okay to steal from the rich if your family is poor?" had answers in the same way that "What is the fifth planet from the Sun?" had an answer. In other words, I believed that there was such a thing as a moral fact.

As I grew older, I encountered the concept of moral relativism. It was profoundly disturbing to think there there might not be objective answers to such important questions; that the universe didn't care. It seemed true but I wanted a way out. I remember the rapper Milo's verse "Open Mike tried to tell me that this is all relative \ But I have found objective truths \ In egg fried rice and fruits".

I kicked and screamed intellectually, but eventually conceded to myself that there was no way moral realism could be compatible with the materialist view of the world I was committed to. I don't remember when this transition actually took place; I suspect it was gradual.
